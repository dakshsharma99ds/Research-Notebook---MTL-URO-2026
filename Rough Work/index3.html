About Me

Hi! I’m a 19-year-old 2nd-year Bachelor of Computer Applications student from India. I started coding on my father’s phone during the pandemic and have since been exploring how computational tools can help understand life sciences, chemistry, and physics. This website documents my first step into research — my journey, learnings, and projects.

My goal: To combine programming skills with scientific research, explore synthetic life, biomolecular transport, and pattern formation, and build tools that help students visualize complex concepts.

Research Journey

Step 1: Curiosity & Exploration

I started asking fundamental questions: What is life? How does it emerge? Can we synthesize it? This led me to follow science news, research articles, and labs working on synthetic biology.

Visual idea: mind maps or doodles of questions and labs explored.

Step 2: Finding Labs & Reading Papers

I explored labs like:

  • Boekhoven Lab – Synthetic life, chemically fueled self-assembly, fuel-dependent droplets
  • Zwicker Group – Biomolecular condensates, droplets in cells, theoretical biophysics simulations

I read papers, made notes, summarized findings, and visualized concepts in my own way.

Step 3: Learning Tools & Programming

I started using Python, AI tools, and frontend development to build simulations and visualization tools that help me understand molecular self-assembly and pattern formation.

Step 4: Connecting Skills to Labs

I identified how my coding skills could contribute:

  • Boekhoven Lab – simulate fuel-driven droplets and self-assembly dynamics
  • Zwicker Group – simulate biomolecular condensates, phase separation, and droplet regulation

Step 5: Building MVP / Simulation

Project: System Simplified

Visualizes operating system concepts like CPU scheduling and molecular self-assembly.

Goal: Help beginners visualize abstract concepts and connect them to real research.
