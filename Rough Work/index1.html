DNA Origami

What is this?

DNA origami is the method of folding DNA into tiny 3D shapes, like nanoscale Lego blocks.

Why study this?

  • Acts as tiny tools for medicine (drug delivery)
  • Can trap viruses
  • Mimics natural molecular machines

What I tried / simulated

  • ✅ Simulated DNA folding as blocks arranging
  • ✅ Built basic 3D shapes in code

Real-Life Applications

  • Virus-trapping cages
  • Nanorobots for targeted drug delivery
  • Artificial molecular machines

DNA-Protein Hybrid Machines

What is this?

Combining DNA and proteins to create nanoscale machines that can move or rotate.

Why study this?

  • Understand molecular motion
  • Develop programmable nanoscale devices

Virus-Trapping Shell

What is this?

Designing nanoscale shells that can capture viruses or particles.

Why study this?

  • To prevent virus infections at the nanoscale
  • Helps understand self-assembly of DNA structures

What I tried / simulated

  • ✅ Built circular shell shapes
  • ✅ Simulated virus particles being trapped

Real-Life Applications

  • Nanoscale cages for virus detection or neutralization
  • Targeted delivery systems for medicine
