About This Website

This website is a personal learning journal to understand and visualize DNA nanotechnology research. It helps me:

  • Translate complex research papers into simple visualizations
  • Experiment with interactive simulations of molecular machines
  • Reflect on learning and explore real-world applications

Purpose: Show initiative, curiosity, and the ability to understand advanced topics despite a non-background start.

Featured Labs & Visualizations

Dietz Lab – DNA Origami

Goal: Design nanoscale 3D shapes using DNA folding techniques.

Papers: Example: “Folding DNA into nanoscale shapes” (Science, 2009)

Simulation: DNA origami folding animation

What I learned: DNA can be folded like Lego blocks into functional nanoscale machines.

Bath Lab – DNA-Protein Hybrids

Goal: Study hybrid molecular machines combining DNA and proteins.

Papers: Example: “Programming DNA-protein motors for nanoscale motion” (Nature Nanotechnology, 2016)

Simulation: Rotating DNA-protein motor

What I learned: Proteins can interact with DNA to produce motion, which can be harnessed for nanoscale transport systems.

Turberfield Lab – Virus-Trapping Shells

Goal: Self-assembling DNA shells to trap viruses and deliver drugs.

Papers: Example: “DNA cages for virus encapsulation” (Nature Chemistry, 2015)

Simulation: Virus-trapping shell animation

What I learned: DNA nanostructures can act as programmable cages for biomedical applications.
